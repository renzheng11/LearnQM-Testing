Screening Effect in Metals and Semiconductors

In the last chapter we saw how negative charges can screen the electric field generated by positive charges and vice versa. We assumed we can put these charges whenever we want. Let's see how these charges appear and distribute in metals and semiconductors and how the screening effect works in these materials. In this chapter we assume the dielectric constant is the same everywhere.

Metals

The density of electrons in metals is extremely high. However, for every free electron there is a positive charge within the metal and the material is initially neutral. Apply a voltage to move charges between the two metallic electrodes and see what happens. You can also change the amount of transferred charge.

Note: the number of electrons and positive charges shown here are representative, and in reality there are many many orders of magnitude more charges involved.
